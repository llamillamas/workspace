## Testing Methodology Decision (Mar 4, 17:15 UTC)

**F1 Master asked:** "Are we actually copying their trades or just simulating?"

**Decision:** **Option A - Paper Test (Simulated)**

**Rationale:**
- Phase 1: Validate strategy concept with zero capital risk
- Phase 2 (if Phase 1 succeeds): Live copy-trading with real execution
- Smart sequence: concept validation → execution proof → scaling

**What Paper Test Does:**
- ✅ Simulates: "If we had copied them at their historical ROI, would we profit?"
- ✅ Validates: Which strategy (A vs B) is theoretically better
- ✅ No risk: No capital at stake, no real trades executed
- ✅ Foundation: Blueprint for Phase 2 live testing

**What Paper Test Does NOT Do:**
- ❌ Real wallet movements
- ❌ Real trade execution
- ❌ Real slippage/fees
- ❌ Actual capital at risk

**Phase 2 (Deferred):**
- Only if Phase 1 (paper test) shows promising strategy
- Would involve: Wallet monitoring → Real trade execution → Actual P&L
- Would require: Solana RPC, real capital, live monitoring

**Files Updated:**
- `FORWARD_TEST_TRACKER.md` — Marked as "SIMULATED"
- `DUAL_FORWARD_TEST.md` — Marked as "PAPER TEST"
- All monitors clarified as paper/simulated

**This is the right approach:** Validate thesis first, prove execution second.
